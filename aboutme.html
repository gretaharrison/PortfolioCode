Hey, my name is Greta Harrison. I’m a UX Designer and I create digital products that are useful, beautiful and intuitive (like this site).

Creating human-centered experiences has been a fundamental theme in my career, from building a brand that resonates with customers, to creating visually enticing digital interfaces based on my studies in human sensory perception. I have an innate fascination with the intersection of science, technology and design - and I love UX because I’m able to explore all of these facets during my design process.

I currently reside in Venice, California, but I’m originally from the foggy hills of Marin County. I moved to Southern California to study Cognitive Science at the University of California, Irvine. In school I was fascinated with the human sensory system and how mental models, motion and design have the power to influence human cognition. After college I tried to blend my Cognitive Science background with my interests in business and design. I joined a start-up in Los Angeles doing Digital Marketing. While I loved my team, I was uninspired by the product and my day to day role. So as a creative outlet and supplemental income stream, I launched an e-commerce based cosmetics company, Mint, in the Fall of 2012. As Mint grew, I was able to make it my full-time priority, and by Summer 2015, I had sold the company.

During a re-design of Mint’s mobile site, I bought my first UX book (The User Experience Team of One by Leah Buley). After reading it cover to cover I was hooked. And even though I was running a company I had built from the ground up, I knew that User Experience Design was my calling.

I may be new to the design world, but I’m eager to learn something new everyday, I’m tech-savvy (hey, I built this site!), and most importantly, as a former business owner I know that good UX is good for business.
